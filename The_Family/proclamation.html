The Proclamation

The Family: A Proclamation to the World was issued in 1995 by the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. This document outlines the Church's teachings regarding the family, marriage, gender, and parental responsibilities. It emphasizes the sacred nature of the family unit and the roles that each member has in contributing to its eternal purpose.

Core Principles

  • Marriage between a man and a woman is ordained of God, and the family is central to the Creator’s plan for the eternal destiny of His children.
  • Gender is an essential characteristic of individual identity and purpose, both in this life and the next.
  • Parents have a responsibility to love and care for each other and their children. Children are a heritage of the Lord, and parents are to teach them to live in righteousness.
  • Fathers and mothers have divine roles, each contributing uniquely to the family structure. Fathers are to preside in love and righteousness, while mothers are primarily responsible for nurturing children.

Scriptural Support

The principles found in the Proclamation align with several scriptures in the Bible and the Book of Mormon. For example, the Bible teaches that "a man shall leave his father and mother, and shall cleave unto his wife" (Genesis 2:24), and the Book of Mormon speaks of the family as a key to God’s eternal plan (see 2 Nephi 2:25). The Proclamation calls for a return to these foundational truths in today's world.

Global Impact and Reactions

Since its release in 1995, the Proclamation has become a central document for understanding the teachings of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints regarding family. It has shaped policies, educational programs, and teachings, both within the Church and in broader discussions about family values in society.

Conclusion

The Family: A Proclamation to the World is a divinely inspired declaration that outlines God's plan for the family. Its teachings are vital for the spiritual, emotional, and physical well-being of individuals and societies. As the world changes, these principles remain eternal and unchanging, offering a clear path to living a life of love, commitment, and divine purpose within the family unit.
