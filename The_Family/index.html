The Proclamation

The Family: A Proclamation to the World, issued by the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in 1995, affirms the divine nature and eternal purpose of the family.

  • Marriage between a man and a woman is ordained of God, and the family is central to the Creator’s plan for the eternal destiny of His children.
  • All human beings—male and female—are created in the image of God. Gender is an essential characteristic of individual premortal, mortal, and eternal identity and purpose.
  • In the premortal realm, spirits lived with God. Mortal life was granted as part of His divine plan.
  • God’s commandment to multiply and replenish the earth remains in force, and sacred powers of procreation are to be employed only between a man and a woman legally and lawfully wedded.
  • Husband and wife have a solemn responsibility to love and care for each other and their children. Children are a heritage of the Lord and are entitled to birth within the bonds of matrimony and to be reared in love and righteousness.
  • Parents are to rear their children in love and righteousness, provide for their physical and spiritual needs, and teach them to love and serve one another, keep God’s commandments, and be law-abiding citizens.
  • Fathers are to preside in love and righteousness, provide necessities of life and protection. Mothers are primarily responsible for the nurture of their children. Fathers and mothers are obligated to help one another as equal partners.
  • Disobedience to these commandments brings consequences, both personal and societal. The disintegration of the family will bring upon individuals and nations the judgments of God.
  • The proclamation ends with a call to responsible citizens and leaders to promote measures that strengthen the family.

Modern Relevance

How the principles in the Proclamation apply today in areas such as parenting, marriage, and gender roles.

The teachings of The Family: A Proclamation to the World continue to offer moral clarity and guidance in a time of shifting social norms. They affirm the value of committed marriage, the sacredness of procreation, and the divine roles of mothers and fathers. As taught by leaders of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, these principles encourage strong family bonds, service, and mutual respect in the home, helping individuals and communities thrive spiritually and socially.
