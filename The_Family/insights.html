Academic Insights

Young Adulthood and Pathways to Eternal Marriage

Recent research indicates that in previous generations, marriage was considered a crucial marker of adulthood. However, today's young people increasingly do not view marriage as necessary for adulthood, focusing instead on personal milestones like financial independence and self-responsibility. This shift is reflected in the rising average age of first marriages. Despite this societal change, studies suggest that a young person’s attitude toward marriage, including the age they expect to marry and the importance they place on it, influences both their lifestyle choices and the long-term success of their marriage and family life. While societal views of marriage continue to evolve, religious teachings consistently emphasize the eternal importance of marriage. The scriptures affirm that marriage is part of God's divine plan, with references from Moses, the Apostle Paul, and modern-day prophets reinforcing its sacredness. The Family Proclamation further underscores that marriage is essential to God's eternal plan for His children. Despite the modern challenges young adults face in today’s dating culture, the need for faith and perseverance in following the prophetic counsel to enter into eternal marriage remains crucial. (Carroll,2013)

Foundational Processes for an Enduring, Healthy Marriage

“The Family: A Proclamation to the World” emphasizes that marriage between a man and a woman is divinely ordained, with husband and wife having a sacred responsibility to love and care for each other. The proclamation underscores that marriage is a vital part of God's eternal plan, and couples have covenant obligations to each other. Elder Bruce C. Hafen (2005) contrasts covenant marriages with contractual ones, highlighting that in a covenant marriage, both partners give 100%, working through challenges together rather than seeking personal benefit. Successful covenant marriages are centered on the teachings of Jesus Christ, and Elder David A. Bednar (2006) illustrates how focusing on Christ strengthens the marital bond. As both partners grow closer to Christ, they grow closer to one another, creating a stronger and more harmonious relationship. (Duncan 2013)

Honoring Marital Vows with Complete Fidelity

President Ezra Taft Benson once warned that the plague of sexual immorality would be one of the greatest challenges of our generation. The teachings of The Family: A Proclamation to the World reinforce this message, emphasizing that the sacred powers of procreation are reserved for a lawful marriage between a man and a woman. The seventh commandment, "Thou shalt not commit adultery," is one that is frequently disregarded in today’s world, yet remains essential for the sanctity of marriage. Marriage is based on total allegiance and fidelity, not just the avoidance of sexual infidelity. Total fidelity involves giving oneself entirely to one’s spouse, as taught by President Spencer W. Kimball. (Gardner and Greiner 2013)

Parenting in Gospel Context: Practices Do Make a Difference

The family proclamation emphasizes the significant role of parents in shaping the lives of their children. It teaches that the ideal environment for a child's growth is one where parents are committed to gospel principles and work to foster their children's development. The home is identified as the foundation for a righteous life, with no other institution able to fully fulfill this responsibility. The proclamation addresses debates about parental influence, particularly in light of emerging research suggesting that genetics and peer groups may be more influential than parents in shaping a child's outcomes. However, research by scholars like Diana Baumrind challenges this view, asserting that different parenting practices lead to varied outcomes. She argues that parents' active involvement, through conscientious socialization, is crucial to healthy child development. The proclamation's counsel aligns with these insights, highlighting specific practices and principles for parents to follow in rearing children. It teaches that teaching children to love and serve one another, among other gospel principles, is key to fostering positive behavior. While genetics may influence behavior, the gospel teachings offer a framework for parents to guide their children in developing desirable qualities and counteracting negative tendencies. This emphasis on active and intentional parenting is consistent with the proclamation’s call for parents to devote their best efforts to their children's spiritual and emotional development. (Nelson 2013)

Repentance and Forgiveness in Family Life

Repentance and forgiveness are intertwined concepts that are essential for healthy relationships, particularly in families. Both processes involve personal and interpersonal aspects: repentance motivates forgiveness, while forgiveness encourages repentance. In family dynamics, these processes are particularly impactful as they strengthen bonds between family members and foster mutual growth. Forgiveness is influenced by several factors, including the intention behind the offense, the relationship between the offender and victim, and individual personality traits such as agreeableness, emotional empathy, and conscientiousness. Additionally, three types of forgiveness are identified: mutual forgiveness (where both parties share fault), bilateral forgiveness (when only one party wrongs the other), and unilateral forgiveness (where one party forgives without an apology). Family situations often requiring forgiveness include infidelity, abuse, and issues like financial irresponsibility or mental illness. From a religious perspective, repentance and forgiveness are foundational to the gospel. The principle of repentance, as taught by ancient prophets like King Benjamin, is central to salvation and emphasizes personal transformation. Similarly, forgiveness is not just a relational tool but a commandment from God, with the New Testament teaching that forgiveness is necessary for receiving forgiveness from God. Thus, both repentance and forgiveness play crucial roles in fostering emotional well-being and spiritual growth within families. (Walton and Hendricks 2013)

Faith in Family Life

Praying together as a family and engaging in religious rituals are powerful ways to strengthen familial bonds and deepen spiritual connections. Family prayer, particularly when approached with humility and a focus on seeking divine guidance, has been shown to enhance emotional validation, foster better conflict resolution, and increase empathy and accountability. When couples pray together, especially in a spirit of love and understanding, it helps to resolve disagreements and brings a sense of peace into the home. In addition to prayer, shared religious rituals such as family worship or observing religious holidays also contribute significantly to marital satisfaction and family closeness. Rituals, like the Jewish Sabbath or Latter-day Saint family home evening, help families transition from everyday life to a sacred space, strengthening both familial and spiritual ties. These practices provide families with meaningful time to focus on their relationship with each other and with God. Regular conversations about faith between parents and children also play a vital role in fostering a strong parent-child relationship. Positive, loving behavior from parents helps children develop an understanding of a loving and supportive God. These discussions, especially those centered around religion, enhance the quality of the parent-child bond. Fathers and mothers both play crucial roles in shaping their children's spiritual understanding, with fathers particularly influencing their children’s perceptions of God. Overall, engaging in religious practices together helps families grow closer, both to each other and to God. (Marks, Dollahite and Freeman 2013)

Wholesome Family Recreation: Building Strong Families

Research on family recreation highlights the importance of wise recreational choices to promote family well-being. Wholesome recreation positively impacts marriage, child development, and adolescent growth. Studies show that marital satisfaction is enhanced through joint recreational activities that involve communication and cooperation, as opposed to parallel or independent activities. Supporting each other’s individual recreational pursuits also contributes to marital satisfaction. For children, engaging in recreational activities with parents fosters positive development, including emotional security and independence. Variety and novelty in recreation are key for nurturing children's curiosity and skills. For adolescents, family recreation can strengthen relationships, promote healthy identity development, and reduce the risk of negative behaviors like delinquency. Research shows that providing diverse, challenging activities helps teens form a positive self-identity and resist peer pressure. Positive psychology also plays a role in wholesome recreation, with leisure activities promoting character strengths such as kindness, perseverance, and gratitude. These strengths are associated with positive outcomes like academic success and leadership. By addressing barriers like lack of time or resources, families can support teens in engaging in meaningful, enriching recreational experiences. (Widmer and Taniguchi 2013)

Defending the Sanctity of Human Life

Life is defined as a state of regenerative activity, energy, and power that all living beings experience. It is not just a biological condition but also an empowerment that persists despite challenges. Emily Dickinson’s quote, “To be alive—is Power,” reflects this perspective. Life is sacred because Jesus Christ, the source of life, identifies himself as "the way, the truth, and the life" (John 14:6). Sacredness is also connected to the union of body and spirit, which allows individuals to experience fullness of joy. Respect for life, from conception to resurrection, is fundamental in Christian teaching, as emphasized by Elder Russell M. Nelson. Life is a divine gift from Heavenly Father, and it must be cherished and protected. Children, in particular, are seen as beloved spirits, each with a divine nature and purpose. Reverence for life deepens when one recognizes the miracle of human existence and the sacred nature of every individual life. (Hallen 2013)
